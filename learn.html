Understanding Ocean Pollution

Ocean pollution happens when harmful materials like plastic, chemicals, and waste enter seas, rivers, and coastal areas. A large portion of this pollution comes from everyday human activities such as improper trash disposal, littering, and overuse of single-use plastics.

Common Types of Marine Debris

Microplastics: These are tiny plastic particles less than 5mm in size. They are often invisible to the naked eye but are found in the deepest parts of the ocean.

Chemical Runoff: Fertilizers and pesticides from farms wash into rivers, creating "dead zones" where oxygen levels are too low for fish to survive.

Ghost Gear: Abandoned fishing nets and lines continue to "fish" on their own, trapping marine life for decades.

Effects on Marine Life

Ingestion: Animals like fish and turtles mistake plastic for food.

Entanglement: Marine animals get trapped in nets, bags, or debris.

Habitat destruction: Coral reefs and ecosystems are damaged, disrupting the entire marine food chain.

The Plastic Timeline

It takes a long time for common items to disappear from our waters:

  • Plastic Straw: 200 years
  • Plastic Bottle: 450 years
  • Fishing Line: 600 years

How You Can Help

Reduce Single-Use: Swap plastic bags for reusable ones and carry a refillable water bottle.

Proper Disposal: Ensure all waste is recycled or binned correctly to prevent it from reaching storm drains.

Spread Awareness: Educate others on the importance of clean waterways. Small changes in habits lead to big impacts for our planet.
